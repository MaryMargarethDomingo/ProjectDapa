Before Fire

1. Have an Emergency Kit Ready

- Maintain emergency food, water and other supplies, including a flashlight, a portable battery-operated radio, extra batteries, medicines, first aid kit and clothing

2. Have a Fire Escape Plan
  

- Your plan in the case of a fire should largely consist of an evacuation plan that youâ€™ve practiced. Make sure you have multiple ways out of each room

3. Make sure you can open windows, screens, security bars, and doors.

- Everyone in your home should be comfortable opening security bars on windows, screen doors, anything that might slow down your exit from a burning building.

4. Make sure you have smoke alarms installed and you change their batteries regularly

- Learn how to shut off gas, water and electricity in case the lines are damaged

5. Keep a List of Emergency Contacts

- Choose an out-of-area friend or relative who separated family members can call after the quake to report their whereabouts and condition

6. Keep household fire extinguishers handy.
 

- A household extinguisher can help keep a small fire from spreading, or help you clear a path to a safe area
